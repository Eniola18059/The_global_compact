IMPPERA Environmental Affairs & Climate Policy
International Marine Plastic Pollution Prevention, Enforcement & Environmental Restoration Framework
Environmental Protection · Marine Conservation · Sustainable Development

An international environmental policy proposal advocating strengthened cooperation, environmental enforcement mechanisms, marine pollution prevention measures, ecological restoration initiatives, and sustainable environmental governance to address the global challenge of marine plastic pollution.

IMPPERA recognizes that marine plastic pollution represents one of the most urgent environmental crises of our time — threatening ocean ecosystems, marine biodiversity, food security, and human health worldwide. It calls for coordinated international action grounded in shared responsibility and sustainable governance.

Policy Objectives
Strengthened international cooperation on marine pollution prevention
Development and enforcement of environmental protection mechanisms
Ecological restoration initiatives for degraded marine ecosystems
Sustainable environmental governance frameworks for ocean stewardship
International research and knowledge-sharing on marine conservation
Community and stakeholder engagement in environmental protection

NCTI Peace, Strategic Stability & Global Cooperation
Nuclear Civilization Transition Initiative
From Deterrence to Cooperation · From Fear to Future

A strategic policy initiative encouraging progressive international dialogue on nuclear risk reduction, strategic stability, peaceful coexistence, confidence-building measures, responsible security governance, and the long-term transition from doctrines of mutual deterrence toward frameworks of cooperation, shared security, human survival, and sustainable global peace.

NCTI holds that human civilization should progressively reduce its dependence upon doctrines rooted in existential fear and mutual destruction, and advance toward a cooperative model grounded in trust, shared security, and responsible stewardship of global peace.

Policy Objectives
Progressive international dialogue on nuclear risk reduction
Confidence-building measures among nuclear and non-nuclear states
Strategic stability frameworks grounded in cooperation not deterrence
Responsible security governance and arms-control cooperation
Long-term transition toward frameworks of shared security
Support for international disarmament dialogue and trust-building

GSEI Peace, Security & Human Development
Global Security Employment Initiative
Public Safety · Employment Generation · Human Security

A policy recommendation encouraging governments, regional organizations, and international institutions to explore accountable, community-oriented security-sector development as a means of strengthening public safety, generating employment opportunities, promoting social stability, and advancing human development.

GSEI recognizes the intersection between security and human development — that communities which are safer, more employed, and more stable are also more peaceful. It promotes security-sector growth as a sustainable engine for employment and social cohesion.

Policy Objectives
Community-oriented security-sector development frameworks
Employment generation through accountable security institutions
Strengthening public safety and community resilience
Promoting social stability through human security investment
Advancing human development alongside security governance
Regional and international cooperation on security-development nexus

UNGFSAII Food Security, Agriculture & Sustainable Development
United Nations Global Food Security & Agricultural Investment Initiative
Food Security · Agricultural Development · Economic Resilience

A cooperative international policy proposal designed to strengthen global food security, agricultural investment, rural transformation, climate-resilient farming systems, and sustainable economic development. The Initiative encourages enhanced national and international collaboration to promote food sovereignty, agricultural innovation, employment creation, and environmental stewardship.

UNGFSAII recognizes resilient food systems as essential foundations for peace, human security, and long-term global stability — affirming that hunger and food insecurity are not only humanitarian crises but also drivers of conflict, displacement, and social fragmentation.

Policy Objectives
Strengthening global food security through international cooperation
Agricultural investment, innovation and rural transformation
Climate-resilient farming systems and sustainable practices
Food sovereignty and agricultural self-sufficiency
Employment creation through agricultural development
Environmental stewardship in food production systems

GSPF Peacebuilding & Responsible Security Governance
Global Security & Peace Stabilization Framework
Peacebuilding · Conflict Prevention · Responsible Security Governance

A comprehensive peacebuilding and conflict prevention framework promoting structured pathways toward sustainable international peace, responsible security governance, and constructive engagement among governments, regional organizations, and international institutions.

GSPF recognizes that sustainable peace requires more than the absence of armed conflict — it demands accountable governance, inclusive political processes, community resilience, and sustained international cooperation. It provides policy guidance for conflict prevention, early warning, mediation, and post-conflict stabilization.

Policy Objectives
Peacebuilding and sustainable conflict prevention frameworks
Responsible security governance and institutional accountability
Early warning systems and preventive diplomacy mechanisms
Mediation, dialogue, and conflict resolution support
Post-conflict stabilization and community resilience
International cooperation on peace and security challenges

Diplomatic Instruments

Policy Proposals &
International Instruments

In addition to the five flagship initiatives, the Global Compact has established formal diplomatic instruments to facilitate voluntary state and institutional engagement.

Diplomatic Instrument A

Member State Nomination Instrument

A voluntary diplomatic instrument through which sovereign states may be formally invited to engage with the Global Compact in a spirit of dialogue, partnership, and constructive international engagement.

Diplomatic Instrument B

Member State Endorsement Instrument

A voluntary instrument enabling sovereign states to express support for the principles and objectives of the Global Compact without creating legal obligations, treaty commitments, or transfer of sovereign authority.

Publication & IP

Authorship, Copyright & Publication Notice

Official authorship, intellectual property, citation, and usage guidance governing policy recommendations, diplomatic instruments, and institutional publications issued under the auspices of the Global Compact.
