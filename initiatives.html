IMPPERA Environmental Affairs & Climate Policy
International Marine Plastic Pollution Prevention, Enforcement & Environmental Restoration Framework
Environmental Protection · Marine Conservation · Sustainable Development

Recognizing the growing scientific evidence of severe plastic contamination throughout the world's oceans, rivers, coastal environments, and marine ecosystems, IMPPERA calls upon international and regional institutions to strengthen environmental governance, promote coordinated ecological protection mechanisms, and advance effective international accountability standards.

Marine plastic pollution and microplastic contamination present significant threats to marine biodiversity, human health, food security, maritime sustainability, climate stability, coastal economies, fisheries, tourism, and future generations. IMPPERA has been formally submitted for the consideration of the United Nations, European Union, African Union, ASEAN, the Commonwealth, ECOWAS, the G20, and the United Kingdom.

Policy Objectives
Eliminate unlawful marine plastic pollution globally
Establish effective international environmental compliance standards
Strengthen accountability mechanisms for large-scale ecological contamination
Enhance environmental law-enforcement cooperation through Environmental Protection Task Forces and Marine Pollution Surveillance Units
Promote responsible industrial plastic production, disposal practices and corporate environmental responsibility
Advance ecological restoration through a proposed International Ecological Restoration Fund

NCTI Peace, Strategic Stability & Global Cooperation
Nuclear Civilization Transition Initiative
Responsible Nuclear Stewardship · Cooperative Security · Strategic Stability

NCTI is founded on the principle that long-term global security is strengthened through responsible leadership, confidence-building, mutual trust, scientific cooperation, effective diplomacy, and full respect for the sovereignty and equality of all States. It has been submitted to the UN, UN Security Council, IAEA, EU, AU, ASEAN, the Commonwealth, ECOWAS, the G20, and the Conference on Disarmament.

The Initiative encourages governments to expand peaceful international engagement, responsible scientific collaboration, institutional resilience, strategic transparency where appropriate, and cooperative security initiatives that reduce risk while promoting sustainable development — advancing a long-term transition from doctrines of mutual deterrence toward frameworks of shared security.

Policy Objectives
Strengthen responsible international nuclear stewardship and safety cooperation
Promote peaceful international cooperation and strategic stability
Encourage confidence-building measures and crisis communication mechanisms
Support peaceful applications of nuclear science — medicine, agriculture, clean energy and environmental monitoring
Strengthen scientific exchange through a proposed International Nuclear Civilization Transition Fund
Advance risk reduction consistent with existing international legal obligations

GSEI Employment, Human Security & Economic Resilience
Global Security Employment Initiative
Decent Work · Workforce Development · Human Security

GSEI is founded on the principle that meaningful employment is fundamental to human dignity, social stability, economic prosperity, and lasting peace. Sustainable employment strengthens national resilience, promotes responsible governance, reduces poverty, supports social cohesion, and contributes to preventing insecurity, violent extremism, organized crime, irregular migration, and humanitarian vulnerability.

The Initiative has been submitted for consideration by the UN, EU, AU, ASEAN, the Commonwealth, ECOWAS, the G20, the International Labour Organization, the World Bank Group, the IMF, and Regional Development Banks — encouraging cooperation among governments, employers, workers' organizations, academia, and civil society to promote decent work and inclusive economic opportunity.

Policy Objectives
Promote sustainable employment opportunities across all sectors of national economies
Strengthen workforce development, vocational education and lifelong learning
Advance youth employment, entrepreneurship and women's economic empowerment
Encourage responsible public-private partnerships and investment for job creation
Strengthen inclusive labour markets, social protection and equal opportunity
Support an International Employment Cooperation, Capacity-Building and Development Fund

DESA Peace, Security, Governance & Cooperative Policy
Defense and Enhancement of Sovereign Autonomy
Cooperative Security · Sovereign Equality · Institutional Resilience

Recognizing the evolving nature of global security challenges — geopolitical tensions, humanitarian emergencies, transnational crime, cyber threats, terrorism, climate-related risks, and emerging technologies — DESA invites international and regional institutions to consider a cooperative framework that strengthens peaceful collaboration while fully respecting the sovereignty, independence, territorial integrity, and equality of all States.

Rather than a military alliance, DESA is presented as a voluntary global policy framework intended to complement existing cooperation mechanisms — strengthening sovereign equality, peaceful engagement, institutional resilience, and collaborative responses to shared global challenges through dialogue and mutually agreed arrangements consistent with international law.

Policy Objectives
Promote sovereign equality, territorial integrity and non-interference among all participating States
Strengthen cooperative security dialogue on terrorism, piracy, organized crime and cybercrime
Support conflict prevention, mediation and peaceful dispute resolution
Enhance institutional resilience, capacity-building and preventive diplomacy
Advance resilient, inclusive economic growth and responsible investment
Strengthen human security through education, healthcare and disaster risk reduction

UNGFSAII Food Security, Agriculture & Sustainable Development
United Nations Global Food Security & Agricultural Investment Initiative
Food Security · Agricultural Development · Economic Resilience

As populations increase, climate variability intensifies, and global supply chains grow more vulnerable, agriculture has become a matter not only of economic development but of national security, human security, and peace. UNGFSAII presents a comprehensive diplomatic and investment framework designed to assist Member States in strengthening national food systems through sustainable public investment, strategic partnerships, and responsible international cooperation.

The Initiative recommends that participating Member States progressively allocate not less than 10% of annual national public expenditure toward agriculture, food security, rural transformation, climate-smart agriculture, value chains, processing, research and innovation.

Policy Objectives
Strengthen global food security through coordinated international cooperation
Mobilize agricultural investment, innovation and rural transformation
Build climate-resilient farming systems and sustainable irrigation
Advance food sovereignty and agricultural self-sufficiency
Create employment through agro-industrial and mechanisation development
Establish strategic national grain reserves and emergency food security systems

GSPF Peacebuilding & Responsible Security Governance
Global Security & Peace Stabilization Framework
Peacebuilding · Conflict Prevention · Responsible Security Governance

A comprehensive peacebuilding and conflict prevention framework promoting structured pathways toward sustainable international peace, responsible security governance, and constructive engagement among governments, regional organizations, and international institutions.

GSPF recognizes that sustainable peace requires more than the absence of armed conflict — it demands accountable governance, inclusive political processes, community resilience, and sustained international cooperation. It provides policy guidance for conflict prevention, early warning, mediation, and post-conflict stabilization.

Policy Objectives
Peacebuilding and sustainable conflict prevention frameworks
Responsible security governance and institutional accountability
Early warning systems and preventive diplomacy mechanisms
Mediation, dialogue, and conflict resolution support
Post-conflict stabilization and community resilience
International cooperation on peace and security challenges

Diplomatic Instruments

Policy Proposals &
International Instruments

In addition to the six flagship initiatives, the Global Compact has established formal diplomatic instruments to facilitate voluntary state and institutional engagement.

Diplomatic Instrument A

Member State Nomination Instrument

A voluntary diplomatic instrument through which sovereign states may be formally invited to engage with the Global Compact in a spirit of dialogue, partnership, and constructive international engagement.

Diplomatic Instrument B

Member State Endorsement Instrument

A voluntary instrument enabling sovereign states to express support for the principles and objectives of the Global Compact without creating legal obligations, treaty commitments, or transfer of sovereign authority.

Publication & IP

Authorship, Copyright & Publication Notice

Official authorship, intellectual property, citation, and usage guidance governing policy recommendations, diplomatic instruments, and institutional publications issued under the auspices of the Global Compact.
