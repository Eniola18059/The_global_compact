Message from the Founder & Global Convener

A Call to Shared
Responsibility & Cooperation

"The Global Compact for Peace, Human Security and Responsible International Cooperation was conceived from a simple yet profound conviction: that humanity's shared challenges require shared responsibility, principled leadership, and constructive cooperation across nations, cultures, institutions, and generations."

At a time when the world faces growing geopolitical tensions, humanitarian crises, environmental challenges, technological transformation, and increasing uncertainty, there is an urgent need for frameworks that encourage dialogue, promote human security, strengthen ethical governance, and inspire collective action.

This Compact is offered as a voluntary multipolar framework for cooperation and partnership. It is founded upon the belief that peace is not merely the absence of conflict, but the presence of justice, dignity, opportunity, responsibility, and mutual respect among peoples and nations.

The vision of this Compact is not to replace existing institutions, but to complement and strengthen efforts that advance peace, sustainable development, human security, and responsible international cooperation. It seeks to provide a platform through which governments, international organizations, academic institutions, civil society, faith communities, youth leaders, private sector partners, and global citizens may work together toward common objectives.

Through the Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy, this initiative further encourages conscience-minded leadership, ethical statecraft, and principled decision-making in public life and international affairs. The doctrine serves not as a political ideology or legal obligation, but as a philosophical call to integrity, wisdom, compassion, restraint, and responsibility.

We live in an era increasingly defined by interdependence and multipolarity. The challenges confronting humanity transcend borders and require cooperation that respects sovereignty, diversity, and the legitimate aspirations of all peoples and nations. This Compact seeks to contribute to a more balanced, inclusive, and cooperative international order grounded in dialogue, mutual respect, shared responsibility, and the common good.

I invite all stakeholders who share these aspirations to engage with this Compact in a spirit of partnership, goodwill, and constructive cooperation. Together, we can contribute to building a more peaceful, secure, just, resilient, and sustainable future for present and future generations.

May this Compact serve as a platform for understanding where there is division, cooperation where there is uncertainty, and hope where there are challenges. Let it stand as a testament to our collective commitment to peace, human dignity, responsible governance, and the shared future of humanity.

H.E. Tunde Atayeshe D. Owolabi (Reign I)

The Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy

A Philosophical Legacy
for Global Order

Conceived by the Founder as the constitutional morality of the Global Compact, the Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy serves as an ethical and philosophical framework for responsible international engagement in an increasingly interconnected and multipolar world.

Diplomacy is not merely the management of relations among states; it is the universal architecture of coexistence through which humanity negotiates order, balance, justice, and peaceful continuity.
Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy — H.E. Tunde Atayeshe D. Owolabi (Reign I)

The Doctrine is not a political ideology, partisan programme, treaty authority, or coercive institutional doctrine. Rather, it functions as a constitutional morality framework for cooperative global order, ethical diplomacy, responsible sovereignty, balanced international engagement, and constructive multilateral cooperation.
