Forum Purpose

The Principal International
Convening Platform

The Annual Global Forum serves as the highest-level institutional gathering of the Global Compact — a structured, inclusive, and action-oriented international platform for dialogue, policy exchange, and cooperative engagement.

I
Peacebuilding & DiplomacyAdvancing dialogue, preventive diplomacy, and trust-building among nations and institutions
II

III
Ethical LeadershipPromoting conscience-minded governance and institutional integrity across all sectors
IV

V
Innovation & PartnershipCatalyzing new partnerships, investment frameworks, and cooperative mechanisms

Forum Participants

Who Attends the
Annual Global Forum

The Forum brings together a diverse, multi-stakeholder community of leaders, institutions, and organizations united by shared commitment to peace, human security, and responsible cooperation.

Heads of State & Government

Presidential and prime ministerial offices, heads of government, vice presidents, and senior ministers

United Nations System

UN Secretary-General's office, specialized agencies, funds, programs, and permanent missions

Regional Organizations

AU, EU, ASEAN, OAS, ECOWAS, and other regional bodies and intergovernmental organizations

Development Finance Institutions

World Bank Group, IMF, regional development banks, and sustainable finance institutions

Academia & Research

Universities, research centers, think tanks, policy institutes, and academic networks

Civil Society & NGOs

Civil society organizations, INGOs, humanitarian bodies, and community development institutions

Private Sector & Investors

Global business leaders, institutional investors, entrepreneurs, and impact finance professionals

Youth Leadership Networks

Young leaders, youth organizations, student movements, and future-generation advocates

Programme Components

Forum Programme
Structure

The Annual Global Forum features a rich, multi-format programme designed to maximize dialogue, knowledge exchange, and partnership development across all thematic areas.

Opening
Plenary Sessions

High-level opening and closing plenary sessions featuring keynote addresses from Heads of State, UN officials, and distinguished institutional leaders.

Dialogue
Policy Dialogues

Structured thematic dialogues on peace, human security, environmental stewardship, digital governance, economic cooperation, and strategic stability.

Discussion
Roundtable Sessions

Intimate, expert-led roundtables on specialized topics allowing for deep engagement, knowledge sharing, and collaborative problem-solving.

Next Generation
Youth Forum

A dedicated Youth Forum track enabling young leaders to contribute ideas, engage with senior decision-makers, and shape the Compact's future priorities.

Thematic
Expert Panels

Multi-expert panel discussions on flagship initiatives, cross-cutting issues, and emerging challenges in international cooperation and global governance.

Collaboration
Partnership Sessions

Dedicated sessions for partnership signings, endorsement ceremonies, cooperation agreement formalization, and investment alignment announcements.

Participation Process

How to Participate in
the Annual Global Forum

Participation in the Annual Global Forum is open to all eligible stakeholders. The process is straightforward, voluntary, and non-binding.

01
Expression of Interest

Contact the Global Secretariat to express your institution's interest in Forum participation, specifying your preferred participation category.

02
Accreditation Review

The Secretariat will review your expression of interest and assess alignment with the Forum's principles, objectives, and participation guidelines.

03
Confirmation & Registration

Upon accreditation, formal participation confirmation and registration details will be provided for the upcoming Annual Global Forum session.

04
Forum Participation

Attend plenary sessions, policy dialogues, roundtables, partnership sessions, and youth forums as an accredited Forum participant.

Forum Hosting & Funding

Hosting the Annual
Global Forum

The Annual Global Forum may be hosted by approved institutions and partners. All hosting arrangements are governed by ethical, voluntary, and transparent principles.

Hosting
Host Institution Eligibility

Governments, international organizations, academic institutions, regional bodies, and approved private sector partners may host the Annual Global Forum, subject to Secretariat approval and institutional guidelines.

Funding
Voluntary & Ethical Funding

Forum funding is voluntary, transparent, and ethical in nature. Support may be received through voluntary contributions, partnerships, and mission-aligned institutional support — never through coercive or conditional arrangements.

Reporting
Outcome Documentation

The Global Secretariat issues comprehensive Forum outcome reports, communiqués, and partnership summaries following each Annual Global Forum session, ensuring transparency and institutional accountability.
