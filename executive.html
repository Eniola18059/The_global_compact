Leadership Grounded in
Diplomacy and Service

H.E. Tunde Atayeshe D. Owolabi founded the Global Compact for Peace, Human Security and Responsible International Cooperation to establish a neutral, voluntary platform through which governments, institutions, and stakeholders worldwide could pursue peace, human dignity, and responsible cooperation without binding legal obligation or compromise of sovereignty.

As Founding Chair, President and Global Convener, he provides the institutional vision behind the Compact's flagship initiatives and its guiding philosophy — the Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy — which holds that diplomacy is the universal architecture through which humanity negotiates order, balance, justice, and peaceful continuity.

Headquartered in Lagos and Abuja, Nigeria, his leadership has directed the Compact's outreach across governments, international organizations, academia, civil society, and the private sector, positioning the institution as a multipolar, multi-stakeholder complement to the existing United Nations system.

Founding Chair — Original architect of the Global Compact's institutional structure and founding declaration
President — Chief executive authority over the Compact's Secretariat and institutional operations
Global Convener — Principal convening authority for the Annual Global Forum and diplomatic engagement
Author of the Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy — The Compact's constitutional and ethical framework

Guiding Philosophy

The Principles Behind
the Founder's Vision

The Executive's leadership is guided by the same foundational principles that anchor the Compact's Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy.

01

Sovereign Equilibrium

Balancing the interests of states and stakeholders within a shared global reality, without compromising sovereignty.

02

Ethical Governance

Leadership rooted in integrity, accountability, and transparency across every institutional office.

03

Multipolar Cooperation

Convening governments, institutions, and civil society as equal partners in a voluntary, non-binding framework.

04

Constitutional Morality

Grounding institutional direction in the Doctrine of Divine Diplomacy as the Compact's ethical constitution.
